## Context

The identity graph distinguishes concrete products from concept classes with the `concrete` flag; `membership` satisfies-edges (member → concept) make members fulfill a concept line, exposed to read-time consumers via `satisfiesAmong`. Capture (`ingredient-normalize.ts` → `resolveOne`) disposes queued terms through lexical fast path → cosine floor → classifier confirm; deterministic sub-passes ride the same job (embedding backfill, segment-overflow repair, SKU co-resolution). The edge-audit job runs the structural pre-pass (self-loop sweep + `X::detail → X` guarantee) every tick. Nothing anywhere models a disjunction: "X or Y" mints concrete.

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**
- A disjunctive term can never again become a concrete identity, on any path (capture, classifier canonical, alias re-audit).
- Existing disjunctive concrete nodes converge to abstract concepts organically — the three production families are the acceptance fixtures — and the serrano churn loop quiesces permanently.
- Members satisfy the concept line (`membership` edges, front-door-resolved, self-healing via enqueue) and the matcher never sends a disjunctive phrase to Kroger.
- Everything within existing primitives: no migration, no new job, no new MCP tool.

**Non-Goals:**
- No general synonym/e.g.-phrase handling ("X (or Y)" parentheticals, "X and/or Y") beyond the ` or ` token pattern; no slash-form splitting.
- No matcher pipeline change (no concept-awareness in `MatchDeps`); no pantry-first resolution inside the matcher.
- No re-anchoring of prep details onto disjuncts (no `jalapenos::form-diced` mint) — prep qualifiers strip, per the existing conservative-collapse rule.
- No general fix for the structural-guarantee oscillation class beyond the disjunctive instance (see Risks).

## Decisions

**D1 — Pattern boundary: the token ` or `, nothing else.** In scope: an id/term containing the standalone token `or` (space-delimited; comma-separated lists with a final ` or ` normalize into the same splitter; `, or ` folds to ` or `). Out of scope, with production justification: **and-compounds** are real buyable products (`half and half`, `pecans (halved and pieces)`, seasoning blends — all concrete in prod; `jellies and jams (various)` is a category the classifier already correctly minted abstract) — "and" composes a product, "or" offers alternatives; **slash forms** have zero production instances and collide with protected ratio qualifiers (`80/20`) and size strings — excluded until evidence exists; hyphenated compounds ride the and-compound rationale. A concrete product whose *name* genuinely contains ` or ` is not known to exist; if one appears, `source='human'` pinning wins (the sweep and the gate skip human rows).

**D2 — Detection is deterministic, pre-classifier, post-lexical.** `resolveOne` order becomes: lexical fast path → **disjunction gate** → floor → confirm. Rationale: detection is purely lexical — no judgment to buy; the classifier is the demonstrated defect vector (it minted all three families as "distinct products"); running lexical first lets punctuation variants of an existing disjunction concept resolve SAME with no new mint. The classifier never sees a pattern-matching term, so the disposal spends zero model calls and cannot be mis-disposed. Cost accepted: a non-lexical variant ("serranos or jalapenos") mints a second concept beside an existing one — concepts are cheap open-world hints, both get the same members via D5, and nothing deterministic joins on them; fragmentation here is harmless where concrete fragmentation was not.

**D3 — Disjunct split: head-noun distribution from the final fragment.** Split on ` or ` (after folding `, or `/`,` list separators to ` or `); let `head` = the final fragment's tokens after its first token; any fragment with **fewer tokens than the final fragment** gets `head` appended. Verified against the fixtures: `white or yellow onion` → `white onion` / `yellow onion`; `serrano or jalapeño peppers` → `serrano peppers` / `jalapeño peppers`; `anaheim or cubanelle peppers` → `anaheim peppers` / `cubanelle peppers`; `olive oil or butter` → `olive oil` / `butter` (no distribution — left is not shorter than the final fragment). Known misfire class (`red bell or poblano peppers` → `red bell`, equal token counts): accepted — disjunct terms feed the normal capture funnel where the classifier disposes them conservatively, junk mints are cheap/mergeable/human-overridable, and the member edge simply doesn't materialize until a real node resolves.

**D4 — Capture disposal mints via the standard `Resolution`, id = the cleaned term verbatim.** Node `{ base: term, detail: null, concrete: false, search_term: firstDisjunct(term), embedding: vec }`, edges `[]`, alias term → id, log `outcome: "novel"` with `detail { note: "disjunction_concept", disjuncts: [...] }` — committed through the untouched `commitResolution` (born-stamped alias, queue delete, one batch). No classifier canonical cleanup: the queued term is already lowercased/quantity-stripped, the phrase is a fine concept label, and determinism is the point. Member edges are NOT proposed at mint — D5 is the single owner of membership (one resolution codepath, no duplicated front-door logic, and the disjunct nodes mostly don't exist yet anyway per the spike).

**D5 — The membership reconcile is a capture-job sub-pass and the single owner of member edges + disjunct enqueue.** `reconcileDisjunctions` runs each tick (even on an empty queue) after the segment-overflow repair and before co-resolution (so co-resolution reads post-fold chains). For every surviving `concrete=0` node whose id matches the pattern: split (D3); resolve each disjunct through the FULL front door — exact alias lookup on the lowercased disjunct, then the representative chain (the same resolution reads the job already loads); when it resolves to a surviving id ≠ the concept and no edge of any kind stands between the pair in either direction, insert `member -[membership]→ concept` **born-stamped** via `insertAuditedEdge` and log `edge_restore` with `note: "disjunction_membership"` (the structural-guarantee precedent); when it does not resolve, enqueue the disjunct to `novel_ingredient_terms` (insert-or-ignore, best-effort) — the recipe-projection capture-surface pattern, re-encountered every tick until capture places it. Write-capped per tick (`DISJUNCTION_MAX_WRITES_PER_TICK = 10`, one shared budget across family repairs and edge inserts; 3 families in prod converge in one or two ticks); idempotent; a converged registry plans nothing and the steady state is an in-memory pattern scan over ~575 rows.

**D6 — Sweep shapes: flip, fold, reroot, mint-base — mirroring the segment repair.** The same sub-pass, before the membership step, selects surviving `source='auto'` `concrete=1` nodes whose id matches the pattern (a `::detail` child matches through its base segment) and applies per family, one atomic batch each via a new `applyDisjunctionRepair` (the `repairSegmentOverflow` sibling):
- **flip** — a bare disjunctive node: `UPDATE concrete=0, search_term=firstDisjunct`; log `outcome: "reshape"`, `detail { note: "disjunction_flip" }`.
- **fold** — a child `X::detail` whose base `X` survives: flip `X` (if still concrete), then `mergeIdentities(child, X)` — prep/detail nuance intentionally drops (prep strips to base per the conservative-collapse rule; read-time reasoning still sees the pantry row's display name, e.g. casey's "Diced jalapeños"). Log the merge with `note: "disjunction_child_fold"`.
- **reroot** — the production serrano inversion (`X.representative = X::detail`): clear `X.representative`, set `child.representative = X`, flip `X` abstract — one batch (the `repairSegmentOverflow` reroot shape with the roles swapped to root the family at the BASE, because the base carries the disjunction identity).
- **mint-base** — a child whose base `X` was never minted: insert `X` abstract (embedding NULL → the existing backfill embeds it), point the child at it.

`source='human'` nodes are skipped and counted, never flipped or folded. Self-quiescing by selection predicate: a flipped node fails `concrete=1`, a folded child fails `representative IS NULL` — no new stamp column (the "pattern non-match after flip" no-op check, realized on the concrete flag). Standing satisfies-edges on flipped nodes are left alone (audited open-world hints; `anaheim… -[membership]→ peppers` remains valid concept-to-concept). Aliases keep resolving to the flipped/folded survivors unchanged; the in-flight alias-target-convergence step later rewrites stored targets of folded children (benign, complementary, no ordering requirement).

**D7 — The general oscillation guard (architect-ratified amendment), plus churn quiescence by shape.** The structural guarantee's (b) step gains one deterministic condition: it skips the insert whenever `resolve(base) === resolve(detail-node)` — it never guarantees an edge that is currently a representative-resolved self-loop. This closes the whole base-into-own-child inversion class, not just the disjunctive instance: on an inverted family the pre-pass (a) sweeps the stamped self-loop edge once and (b) no longer re-inserts it, so the churn quiesces even BEFORE the shape sweep runs. The sweep then still converges the shape itself: after the fold/reroot, `X::form-diced` no longer survives, the guarantee never re-engages for it, and the family is structurally correct — not merely quiet. The guard is one `continue` in `ensureStructuralEdges`; healthy families (a surviving detail node whose base resolves elsewhere) are untouched.

**D8 — Matcher: no code change; the guarantee moves into `search_term`.** An abstract disjunction node always carries `search_term` = first disjunct (set at mint by D4, at flip by D6). The existing pipeline then: searches Kroger on a member phrase (never the ` or ` phrase; also removes the `or` relevance-token poisoning); with no cache row and no `[brands]` key returns **ambiguous** with the full relevance-ranked candidate set — which IS the normalization spec's "members surfaced as options rather than a silent auto-pick"; a user-confirmed choice caches under the concept id, a learned group resolution revalidated on every later hit. Alternatives rejected: member-with-sku-cache-hit fallback (needs location context and member enumeration inside the matcher — real `MatchDeps` surface growth for zero production benefit today) and `concepts` set in `MatchDeps` (same). Production carries zero concept-keyed cache/brand rows, so there is no legacy state to migrate. First-disjunct is deterministic and stable (derived from the id); it is not updated later when members gain cache entries (evidence-gated future work).

**D9 — Concept–concrete merge guards at the two merge-proposing passes, not in `mergeIdentities`.** (a) Co-resolution: skip a pair whose survivors differ in `concrete` (counted `mergeSkipped`; `CoResolutionPair` gains the flag from the reader's existing identity read) — once concept-keyed sku_cache rows exist (D8), member and concept legitimately co-resolve to one SKU and must never be merge-proposed. (b) Re-confirm: a `same` pick whose survivor is a concept node rejects to a logged no-op (stamp, change nothing) — the serrano inversion's entry path. The guard is NOT placed in `mergeIdentities` itself: D6's fold deliberately merges a (transiently concrete) child into an abstract base, and the alias-audit's stranded-orphan merge may legitimately fold a junk mint into a concept target — the primitive stays general, the proposing passes carry the judgment.

**D10 — Classifier belt-and-braces: one prompt rule + a deterministic canonical rejection + re-audit parity.** The confirm prompt gains: a term or canonical of the form "X or Y" is a purchase constraint, never a concrete product — do not mint or match it as one (no new few-shot; the deterministic gate is the real defense). `buildResolution`'s NOVEL branch rejects a classifier-proposed canonical matching the pattern (`canonical_reason: "disjunctive"`, verbatim fallback — safe because a disjunctive queued term never reaches `buildResolution`). The alias re-audit adds the same deterministic branch the capture gate has: an eligible disjunctive-pattern variant is disposed with no model call — re-pointed to its disjunction concept (minted via the D4 shape if absent, the variant's embedding is already in hand), stamped, logged with the audit marker. Production's disjunctive aliases are all already audited; the branch exists for other operators' backlogs and for rule parity ("the fast path applies at capture and at the alias re-audit alike" precedent).

**D11 — No schema change (challenged).** Candidate columns and why they're not needed: a "disjunction" marker — derived from the id pattern, deterministically recomputable; a sweep stamp — quiescence is by selection predicate (D6); a disjunct list — recomputed by the splitter (D3), and storing it would rot; rejection memory — nothing here re-asks a model. The queue, log, edge table, and `concrete` flag are reused as-is. Confirmed: zero migrations.

**D12 — Observability: additive `NormalizeSummary` fields.** `disjunctionCaptured` (capture disposals), `disjunctionFlipped`, `disjunctionFolded` (fold + reroot + mint-base), `disjunctionEdges`, `disjunctionEnqueued`, `disjunctionSkipped` (human) — flowing into the existing `job_health`/`job_runs` summaries and admin rendering with no admin code change (the panel renders summaries generically; the Audits tab's per-pass field enumerations are for the audit/sku passes, which this change does not touch — verified against `audit-admin.ts`, which the in-flight change is also editing). New log outcome value `reshape` is additive TEXT; the Decisions stream renders outcome strings as-is and filters only `edge_*`.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- **Splitter misfires on equal-token disjuncts** (`red bell or poblano peppers` → `red bell`): mitigated by routing disjuncts through the normal conservative capture funnel; a misfired term becomes at worst a cheap novel mint, later mergeable, and the member edge simply doesn't form. Not silently wrong-purchase-shaped.
- **Concept fragmentation from non-lexical disjunction variants** (D2): two concepts with the same members; harmless to joins (nothing deterministic keys across concepts), invisible to the matcher (each carries a member search_term), and human-collapsible.
- **The `…::form-diced` fold discards the "diced" nuance:** casey's pantry "Diced jalapeños" resolves to the plain disjunction concept. Accepted: prep qualifiers strip to base by standing rule, the display name still carries the nuance for read-time reasoning, and no sku_cache row is lost (none exists).
- **A guarded inverted family is quiet but not yet correct** until its shape converges: the D7 guard stops the churn for ANY base-into-own-child inversion, while only disjunctive families get their shape repaired by the sweep. Accepted — the guard makes the defect harmless (no writes, no log noise), D9(b) closes the known entry path (re-confirm `same` onto a concept), and a hypothetical future non-disjunctive inversion converges the day a shape-owning change addresses it.
- **Doc-file overlap with the in-flight `alias-target-convergence` change** (`docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`, `docs/SCHEMAS.md`; both also near `audit-admin.ts`): different sections, textual merge only; implementation serializes after that change lands (working-mode rule), and its alias-retarget step composes correctly with D6's folds (it rewrites stored alias targets of merged-away children — key maintenance this change relies on it for).
- **`reshape` log rows have no replay/audit machinery:** deliberate — they are deterministic shape maintenance, not decisions; the summary counts are the audit trail (the sku-rekey precedent).

## Migration Plan

None (no schema change). Rollout is the deploy itself: the first post-deploy capture tick flips `white or yellow onion` and `anaheim or cubanelle peppers`, reroots + flips the serrano base and folds its child, sets member search_terms, enqueues the six disjunct terms; subsequent ticks capture the disjuncts and the membership reconcile links them. The churn loop stops within one edge-audit tick after the fold (one final self-loop sweep). Grocery/pantry `normalized_name` and stored alias targets converge through the existing reconciles. Verification is the tasks' production fixture walk. Rollback is a standard worker rollback; flipped rows are semantically valid under pre-change code (concrete=0 nodes already existed; `search_term` remains a valid search phrase).
